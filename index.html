Mahatma Gandhi

2 Oct. 1869 - 30 Jan. 1948

Father of the Nation

Mahatma Gandhi, born Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi on Octomber 1869 in Porbandar, Gujarat India. He was a pivotal figure in India's struggle for independence from British rule. He led the fight for freedom through his philosophy of non-violence and truth, known as Satyagraha. Gandhi's influence extended beyond India, inspiring movements for civil rights and equality worldwide. Gandhiji studied law in England and later practiced in South Africa, where he faced racial discrimination. This experience fueled his commitment to non-violent resistance and his later advocacy for civil rights. Mahatma Gandhi returned to India in 1915 and became a leader in the Indian National Cogress. He advocated for non-violent resistance to British rule, organizing movements like the Civil Disobedience Movement and the Salt March.

Mahatma Gandhi's leadership was very crucial in achieving India's independence in 1947. Mahatma Gandhi's first nationalwide Satyagraha was too late to influence the framing of the new Government of India Act of 1919, the magnitude of disorder resulting from the movement was unparalleled and presented a new challenge to foreign rule. His popularity soared in Indian politics as he reached the hearts and minds of ordinary people, winning support for his causes as no one else had ever done before. Emerging leaders within the Congress-- Jawaharlal Nehru, Vallabhbhai Patel , Rajedra Prasad, C. Rajagopalachari- accepted Gandhi's leadership in articulating nationalist aspirations but disagreed on strategies for wresting more concessions from the British.

Mahatma Gandhi reemerged from his long seclusion by undertaking his most inspired campaign ,a march of about 400 Kilometers from his commune in Ahmadabad to Dandi , on the coast of Gujarat between March 12 and April 6,1930. At dandi , in protest against extortionate British taxes on salt, he and thousands of followers illegally but symbolically made their own salt from sea water.

Key Movements :-

  • Champaran Satyagraha (1917):

    This was Gandhiji's first major movement in India, focused on the plight of indigo farmers in Bihar, where they were forced to grow indigo for British planters.


  • Non-Cooperation Movement (1920):

    Launched in response to the Jallianwala Bagh massacre, this movement called fr Indians to baycott services. It was a mass movement that mobilized from all walks of life.


  • Civil Disobedience Movement (1930):

    This movement,starting with the Dandi March to protest the salt tax,involved mass civil Disobedience and non-payment of taxes.


  • Quit India Movement (1942):

    Launched during World Wae 2,this movement demanded the immediate withdrawal of British rule from India. It was a call for "Do or Die", urging Indians to fight for independence.
